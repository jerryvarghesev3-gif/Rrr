Your 8 IDP / Performance Goals (Refined)

1. Implement Structured Branching Strategy

Goal:
Adopt and consistently follow a well-defined branching strategy for all development activities.

Actions:
	•	Define branching approach (e.g., feature branch, release branch) with team/lead
	•	Ensure all developments follow the agreed strategy
	•	Reduce issues caused by improper branching

⸻

2. Strengthen Development Testing Practices

Goal:
Improve development quality by ensuring proper testing before task completion.

Actions:
	•	Treat development testing as part of “Definition of Done”
	•	Ensure all use cases are validated before closure
	•	Reduce defects identified in later stages

⸻

3. Improve Stakeholder Collaboration

Goal:
Enhance collaboration with stakeholders to ensure alignment and smooth execution.

Actions:
	•	Communicate regularly with stakeholders
	•	Ensure clarity on requirements and expectations
	•	Take ownership of driving closure with alignment

⸻

4. Enhance Task Planning & Personal Organization

Goal:
Improve planning and tracking of tasks to ensure timely and predictable delivery.

Actions:
	•	Break down work into actionable tasks
	•	Track progress daily and maintain closure timelines
	•	Avoid last-minute escalations or dependency risks

⸻

5. Ensure Consistent Code Review Practices

Goal:
Improve code quality through consistent peer code reviews.

Actions:
	•	Get all code reviewed before merging
	•	Actively participate in reviewing peers’ code
	•	Use reviews to identify and fix defects early

⸻

6. Demonstrate Strong Ownership & Accountability

Goal:
Take end-to-end ownership of assigned tasks and ensure timely closure.

Actions:
	•	Proactively drive tasks to completion
	•	Avoid unnecessary dependency on leads for closure
	•	Take responsibility for outcomes and deliverables

⸻

7. Proactively Seek Help & Improve Team Collaboration

Goal:
Reduce delivery risks by identifying when support is needed and collaborating effectively.

Actions:
	•	Identify tasks that require support early
	•	Seek help proactively instead of delaying
	•	Contribute to overall team efficiency

⸻

8. Improve Task Grooming & Jira Planning

Goal:
Ensure all tasks are properly groomed, planned, and tracked in Jira.

Actions:
	•	Convert all requests (mail/chat) into properly defined Jira tasks
	•	Add clear descriptions, acceptance criteria, and timelines
	•	Maintain accurate tracking with planned closure dates
	•	Avoid starting work without proper planning





9. Embedded Low-Level Driver Development & Design

Goal:
Develop expertise in embedded low-level driver design and implementation for improved system performance and reliability.

Actions:
	•	Understand hardware interfaces (SPI, I2C, UART, GPIO, etc.) used in the project
	•	Design and implement at least 1–2 low-level drivers/modules
	•	Follow best practices for embedded coding (efficiency, memory, timing constraints)
	•	Debug and optimize driver performance on target hardware
	•	Document design and implementation for reuse

Impact:
	•	Strengthens core embedded skills
	•	Improves system-level understanding
	•	Adds high technical value to the team

⸻

✅ 10. Innovation through POC / Invention

Goal:
Drive innovation by developing at least one Proof of Concept (POC) or contributing to an invention/patent.

Actions:
	•	Identify an improvement area (performance, cost, automation, AI, etc.)
	•	Design and implement a POC solution
	•	Demonstrate feasibility and measurable benefits
	•	Document the solution for potential Invention Record (IR) / patent filing
	•	Collaborate with team for innovation initiatives

Impact:
	•	Contributes to organizational innovation targets
	•	Enhances problem-solving and creativity
	•	Builds visibility and recognition
